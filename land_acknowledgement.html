§ Land Acknowledgement

Cornell University is located on the traditional homelands of the Gayoghon{o} (the Cayuga Nation). The Gayoghon{o} are members of the Hodinǫ̱hsǫ́:nih Confederacy, an alliance of six sovereign Nations with a historic and contemporary presence on this land. The Confederacy precedes the establishment of Cornell University, New York state, and the United States of America.

Cornell University acknowledges the painful history of Gayoghon{o} dispossession, and honors the ongoing connection of Gayoghon{o} people, past and present, to these lands and waters. This research project was developed at Cornell, and the author wishes to extend that acknowledgement to the broader work documented here. The Department of Applied Economics and Management sits within the College of Agriculture and Life Sciences, on land that was wrested from the Cayuga Nation through a sequence of treaties beginning in 1789 and that remains the subject of ongoing claims and unfulfilled federal obligations.

This page reproduces the official statement maintained by Cornell's Office of the Vice Provost for Academic Affairs. Readers are encouraged to consult the Gayoghon{o} Learning Project, supported by Cornell, for educational resources, and to support the Cayuga Nation directly.

If you are reading this from elsewhere, the author encourages you to identify and acknowledge the Indigenous peoples whose traditional territory you occupy. Native Land Digital is a useful starting point.
